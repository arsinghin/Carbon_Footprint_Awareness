## Challenge Goal

Build a web-based Carbon Footprint Awareness Platform that helps individuals:

* Understand their carbon footprint
* Track carbon-generating activities
* Reduce their environmental impact
* Develop long-term awareness and behavior change through personalized insights

The objective is not simply to calculate emissions.

The platform should transform carbon data into meaningful awareness that influences future decisions and actions.

---

# Core Problem

Most people:

* Do not know their carbon footprint
* Cannot interpret carbon numbers meaningfully
* Do not understand the impact of daily decisions
* Lack motivation to consistently improve behavior

The solution should bridge the gap between:

Raw Carbon Data → Personal Understanding → Actionable Decisions → Behavior Change

---

# Key Design Principle

## Awareness > Tracking

A carbon calculator alone is insufficient.

The platform should create awareness by helping users understand:

* What activities generate emissions
* Why those emissions matter
* How their choices compare with alternatives
* What actions would reduce impact

The user should leave with better understanding and clearer decisions, not just a score.

---

# Expected User Experience

The platform should provide:

## Understanding

Help users understand:

* Where emissions come from
* Which activities contribute most
* Relative impact of different choices

## Context

Carbon information should be presented with context.

Examples:

* Comparisons
* Equivalencies
* Impact explanations
* Personalized interpretation

Raw numbers without explanation provide limited value.

## Personalization

Insights should adapt based on:

* User behavior
* User activities
* User patterns
* User goals

Generic recommendations are less valuable than personalized guidance.

## Actionability

Users should receive practical suggestions that can realistically reduce emissions.

Recommendations should be:

* Specific
* Relevant
* Easy to understand
* Achievable

---

# Strong Solution Characteristics

A strong solution goes beyond reporting.

Possible characteristics include:

* Decision support
* Context-aware recommendations
* Behavioral nudges
* Progress tracking
* Habit formation support
* Visual feedback systems
* Gamification
* Social accountability mechanisms
* Personalized coaching

The platform should encourage action rather than simply display information.

---

# Smart Assistant Requirement

The solution should behave like a smart assistant.

It should demonstrate:

* Understanding of user context
* Logical decision making
* Personalized reasoning
* Dynamic recommendations

The system should not provide identical outputs for every user.

---

# Practicality Requirement

The platform must solve a real-world problem.

Requirements:

* Useful in everyday life
* Understandable by non-technical users
* Easy to interact with
* Capable of influencing actual decisions

Avoid features that are technically impressive but provide little practical value.

* Well structured

* Modular

* Follow secure development practices

## Efficiency

* Maintain responsive user experience

* Meaningful test coverage
* Functional validation

## Accessibility

* Inclusive

6. Security practices

---

# Design Guidance

When making implementation decisions, prioritize:

1. User awareness
2. Behavioral impact
3. Personalization
4. Practical usefulness
5. Simplicity
6. Maintainability

Avoid building:

* Basic carbon calculators
* Dashboard-only products
* Generic reporting systems
* Features that exist solely for visual appeal without improving awareness or decision making

The platform should help users understand their impact, make better choices, and sustain those improvements over time.
